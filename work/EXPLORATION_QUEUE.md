## Q01: Which single consumer flow best proves “silent until relevant” plus redeemable checkout in under 60 seconds?
**Output path:** work/explore/01-consumer-flow-proof.md
**Why it matters:** The one-minute demo is the highest-leverage artifact, and the product wins only if the consumer story feels precise rather than scripted.
**Approach hint:** Compare candidate demo spines against the brief and seed, then recommend the shortest beat sequence that still proves context detection, dynamic offer generation, and redemption.
**Would falsify:** If no concise flow can prove both relevance and checkout, the consumer-first pitch is too ambitious for the submission format.

## Q02: What second scenario most efficiently proves the engine generalizes beyond Mia’s main path?
**Output path:** work/explore/02-second-scenario-choice.md
**Why it matters:** A strong second scenario can turn the product from a single scripted vignette into a credible platform without blowing the time budget.
**Approach hint:** Evaluate the open seed options and any obvious alternatives against demo clarity, setup cost, and how clearly they show “same engine, different context.”
**Would falsify:** If no second scenario adds clear evidence fast, the pitch should drop the generalization beat and stay single-threaded.

## Q03: Which context signals are actually strong enough to support the claim that offers are grounded in real signals rather than hand-authored coupons?
**Output path:** work/explore/03-signal-claim-audit.md
**Why it matters:** The core innovation claim collapses if weather, event, location, and demand signals cannot be shown as real inputs to offer selection and generation.
**Approach hint:** Audit the staged signal sources and docs at a product level, then separate demo-safe signals from weak or fixture-only signals and recommend the honest claim boundary.
**Would falsify:** If the available signals do not credibly drive different offers, the “AI-native city wallet” framing is overstated.

## Q04: What is the minimum merchant portfolio and approval-rule set that still makes the merchant-side cut feel real?
**Output path:** work/explore/04-merchant-scope-minimum.md
**Why it matters:** Merchant setup scope directly affects build time, while the auto-approve mechanic is one of the few distinctive ideas in the seed.
**Approach hint:** Define the smallest set of merchant categories, example goals, and reusable approval rules needed to support the consumer demo and one merchant callback shot.
**Would falsify:** If the merchant side needs too many bespoke cases to look credible, the two-sided story should be narrowed further.

## Q05: Which submission assets and proof points remain unknown enough to risk a last-minute packaging failure?
**Output path:** work/explore/05-submission-risk-checklist.md
**Why it matters:** Communication is weighted equally, so missing or misunderstood submission constraints can sink an otherwise solid build.
**Approach hint:** Turn the hackathon brief’s knowns and unknowns into a concrete checklist of unanswered asset, format, and narrative requirements that must be resolved before packaging.
**Would falsify:** If the remaining asset requirements are still unclear late in the build, the current submission plan is not execution-safe.
